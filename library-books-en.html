Library: some good books

Just a space to save some cool references I find interesting. I have not read most of them in depth, but I would definitely recommend most of them.


  1. Mathematics for the physical sciences (beyond elementary algebra and calculus):
    • Gelfand, I. M. & Fomin, S. V. (2012). Calculus of variations Newburyport, MA, USA: Dover Publications.
    • Bak, J., & Newman, D. J. (2010). Complex analysis. New York, NY, USA: Springer New York.
    • Hochstadt, H. (2012). The functions of mathematical physics. Newburyport, MA, USA: Dover Publications.
    • Bender, C. M. & Orszag, S. A. (1999). Advanced mathematical methods for scientists and engineers: Asymptotic methods and perturbation theory. New York, NY, USA: Springer New York.

  2. Data analysis:
    • Ivezić, Ž., Connolly, A. J., Vanderplas, J. T., & Gray, A. (2014). Statistics, data mining, and machine learning in astronomy: A practical Python guide for the analysis of survey data. Princeton, MA, USA: Princeton University Press.

  3. Fundamental astronomy and astrophysics:
    • Rybicki, G. B. & Lightman, A. P. (2004). Radiative processes in astrophysics (2nd Ed.). Weinheim, Germany: Wiley VCH.
    • [In Spanish] Clocchiatti, A. & Catelan, M. (2017). Radiación y materia en astrofísica. Santiago, Chile: Ediciones UC.

  4. Stellar astrophysics:
    • Lamers, J. G. L. M. & Levesque, E. M. (2017). Understanding stellar evolution Bristol, UK: IOP Publishing.
    • Kippenhahn, R., Weigert, A., & Weiss, A. (2012). Stellar structure and evolution (2nd Ed.). Berlin Heidelberg, Germany: Springer-Verlag.
    • Tassoul, J.-L. (2000). Stellar rotation. Cambridge, UK: Cambridge University Press.

  5. Physics in general (not general physics):
    • Penrose, R. (2004). The road to relity: A complete guide to the laws of the Universe. New York, NY, USA: Vintage Books.

  6. Mechanics (classical and quantum):
    • Goldstein, H., Safko, J., & Poole, G. (2002). Classical mechanics (3rd Ed.). San Francisco, CA, USA: Addison-Wesley.
    • Pain, H. J. (2002). The physics of vibrations and waves (6th Ed.). Chichester, UK: Wiley.
    • Qiang, Y.-Q., Gu, E.-P., Cheng, J.-F., Li, Z.-H., Yang, D.-T., & Lim, Y.-K. (Ed.) (1994). Major America universities Ph.D. qualifying questions and solutions (Vol. 1, Problems and solutions on mechanics). Singapore: World Scientific Publishing Co. Pte. Ltd.
    • Griffiths, D. J. (2005). Introduction to quantum mechanics (2nd Ed.). Upper Saddle River, NJ, USA: Pearson Prentice Hall.
    • Zhang, Y.-D., Zhu, D.-P., Fang, H.-Y., & Lim, Y.-K. (Ed.) (1998). Major America universities Ph.D. qualifying questions and solutions (Vol. 6, Problems and solutions on quantum mechanics). Singapore: World Scientific Publishing Co. Pte. Ltd.
    • Greiner, W. (1990). Relativistic quantum mechanics Berlin Heidelberg, Germany: Springer-Verlag
    • Peskin, M. E. & Schroeder, D. V. (2018). An introduction to quantum field theory Boca Raton, FL, USA: CRC Press.

  7. Thermodynamics and statistical mechanics:
    • Callen, H. B. (1985). Thermodynamics and an introduction to thermostatistics (2nd Ed.). New York, NY, USA: Wiley.
    • Greiner, W., Neise, L., & Stöcker, H. (1995). Thermodynamics and statistical mechanics New York, NY, USA: Springer-Verlag New York, Inc.
    • Zheng, J.-R., & Lim, Y.-K. (Ed.) (1990). Major America universities Ph.D. qualifying questions and solutions (Vol. 5, Problems and solutions on thermodynamics and statistical physics). Singapore: World Scientific Publishing Co. Pte. Ltd.

  8. Electrodynamics:
    • Jackson, J. D. (1999). Classical electrodynamics (3rd Ed.). New York, NY, USA: John Wiley & Sons.
    • Batygin, V. V. & Toptygin, I. N. (1964). Problems in electrodynamics New York, NY, USA: Academic Press Inc.
    • Zhao, Sh.-P., You, J.-H., Zhu, J.-J., & Lim, Y.-K. (Ed.) (1993). Major America universities Ph.D. qualifying questions and solutions (Vol. 2, Problems and solutions on electromagnetism). Singapore: World Scientific Publishing Co. Pte. Ltd.

  9. Hydrodynamics and plasma physics:
    • Landau, L. D. & Lifshits̅, E. M. (1987). Fluid mechanics (2nd Ed.). Oxford, UK: Elsevier.
    • Chen, F. F. (2016). Introduction to plasma physics and controlled fusion (3rd Ed.). Cham, Switzerland: Springer International Publishing AG Switzerland.
    • Rezzolla, L. & Zanotti, O. (2014). Relativistic hydrodynamics. Oxford, UK: Oxford University Press.
    • Punsly, B. (2008). Black hole gravitohydromagnetics (2nd Ed.). Berlin, Germany: Springer.

  10. Celestial mechanics:
    • Murray, C. D. & Dermott̅, S. F. (1999). Solar system dynamics Cambridge, UK: Cambridge University Press.
    • Binney, J. & Tremaine, S. (2008) Galactic dynamics (2nd Ed.). Princeton, NJ, USA: Princeton University Press.
    • Hagihara, Y. (1970–). Celestial mechanics. Cambridge, MA, USA: MIT Press.
      • Vol. 1, Dynamical principles and transformation theory.
      • Vol. 2, Pts. 1–2, Perturbation theory.
      • Vol. 3, Pts. 1–2, Differential equations in celestial mechanics.
      • Vol. 4, Pts. 1–2, Periodic and quasi-periodic solutions.
      • Vol. 5, Pts. 1–2, Topology of the three-body problem.
    • Boccaletti, D. & Pucacco, G. (1996–). Theory of orbits. Berlin Heidelberg, Germay: Springer-Verlag.
      • Vol. 1, Integrable systems and non-perturbative methods.
      • Vol. 2, Perturbative and geometrical methods.

  11. General relativity:
    • Papapetrou, A. (1974). Lectures on general relativity Dordrecht, Holland.
    • Lightman, A. P., Press, W. H., Price, R. H., & Teukolsky, S. A. (1975). Problem book in relativity and gravitation. Princeton, NJ, USA: Princeton University Press.

  12. Compact objects:
    • Camenzind, M. (2007). Compact objects in astrophysics: White dwarfs, neutron stars, and black holes. Berlin Heidelberg, Germany: Springer-Verlag.

  13. Metaphysics [in Spanish]:
    • Vial Larraín, J. de D. (2005). La vía de la verdad: El discurso de la metafísica. Santiago, Chile: Ediciones UC.
    • Heidegger, M. (2022). El ser y el tiempo (3a Ed.). Ciudad de México, México: Fondo de Cultura Económica.

  14. Botany [mostly in Spanish]:
    • Font Quer, P. (2020). Diccionario de botánica: Una obra imprescindible para todo botánico que se precie. Barcelona, Spain: Ediciones Península.
    • Crang, R., Lyons-Sobaski, S., & Wise, R. (2019). Plant anatomy: A concept-based approach to the structure of seed plants. Cham, Switzerland: Springer Nature Switzerland AG.
    • [Available in open access!] Halbritter, H., Ulrich, S., Grímsson, F., Weber, M., Zetter, R., Hesse, M., Buchner, R., Svovtja, M., & Frosch-Radivo, A. (2018). Illustrated pollen terminology (2nd Ed.). Cham, Switzerland: Springer International Publishing AG. DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-71365-6
    • Muñoz Pizarro, C., Sierra Ráfols, E. (il.), & Sudzuki H., F. (coil.) (1966). Sinopsis de la flora chilena: Claves para la identificación de familias y géneros. Santiago, Chile: Ediciones Universidad de Chile. Available at https://www.chlorischile.cl/index.html.
    • Navas Bustamante, L. E. (1973–1976). Flora de la Cuenca de Santiago. Santiago, Chile: Editorial Universitaria. Available at https://www.chlorischile.cl/index.html.
