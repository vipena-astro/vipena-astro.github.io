Library: some cool online resources

Just a space to save some cool references I find interesting. I have not read most of them in depth, but I would definitely recommend most of them.


  1. Coolors: generate colour palettes interactively
  2. W3 Schools: learn how to code in different programming languages
  3. W3 schools' HTML Color Picker: explore colour spaces interactively
  4. lipsum.com: Dummy text generator
  5. Hyperspace@gu: Blog on general relativity and gravitation (jobs, conferences, etc.)
  6. Wolfram MathWorld: Technical encyclopedia of mathematics
  7. NIST Digital Library of Mathematical Functions.
  8. Heliosesimic and Magnetic Imager (HMI) Science Nuggets: science blog on heliophysics
  9. Resource library for accessibility to astro/physics for people with disabilities:
    • General guidelines and resources by Astronomers Without Borders
    • Resource library at AstroBetter
    • Color Oracle: colour blindness simulator
    • BlindMath: mailing list for topics regarding access to math for blind people
    • latex2nemeth: a software capable of converting LaTeX documents into (Greek) Braille + Nemeth, plus a brief collection of Braille books on Mathematics in Greek and English
